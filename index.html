The data types in the above chart are attributes, specifically
ordinal. The marks are the points for each data point in each
listed year as well the lines connecting them. The marks are
used as nodes (each point) and the lines are used as links. The
channels are the different colors of each line and position on
the axis, both vertical and horizontal. Tilt could also be
considered a channel.

The data types seen in the above chart are items (each node in
the map) and links (the relationships between the nodes). The
marks are the nodes in the map (like points) and the lines
between them. The node marks are used as items and the lines
connecting them are links. The channels are the different
colors and potentially the position of the nodes from the lines that
lead to them.
